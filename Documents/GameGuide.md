Game Overview
Story Line
Sherwood City is alive with tension. Its cracked streets and boarded storefronts have become the battleground for two rising powers, each carving out influence block by block, business by business.
Wavy & Co. (Blue)
Wavy & Co. began as a grassroots surf and skate lifestyle brand and has evolved into a global logistics powerhouse. We bridge the gap between urban culture and international trade, specializing in maritime import/export and "pop-up" retail experiences. From the docks to the streets, Wavy & Co. keeps goods moving and the culture flowing.
________________________________________
The Select Few & Co. (Red)
The Select Few & Co. is a premier boutique consultancy firm specializing in generational wealth preservation and risk mitigation for the world’s most discerning clientele. We handle the logistics of luxury: from securing rare art to managing private security details for executive transport. When exclusivity is paramount, The Select Few is the only choice.________________________________________
The Streets Are the Prize
Every gas station, corner store, scrapyard, and safehouse is up for grabs. Crews will scan and claim territories, shake down businesses, and complete missions to earn Mutant Experience Points and power within the city. 
This isn’t about saving the city.
It’s about owning it.

Basic Game Structure
Scoring System
Mutant Experience Points (MREP)
Mutant Experience Points are the primary measure of your influence, credibility, and power in the streets of Sherwood City. Every mission you complete, every territory you secure, and every prop you turn in earns you MREP, proving your hustle and commitment to your crew.
Unlike currency, which can be spent, MREP is permanent and tracks your legacy throughout the event. The higher your MREP, the more respect is put on your NAME.
Earning MREP:
•	Completing missions (heists, territory control)
•	Turning in props and loot
•	Participating in side hustles
Why MREP Matters:
•	Unlocks event achievements and leaderboard visibility
•	Can grant MREP multipliers with purchased classes
•	Influences end-of-event rewards and prize pools
In Sherwood City, cash might keep you alive, but MREP decides who really runs the streets.

Gameplay Mechanics
Mission Systems
Territory Control Modes
Domination Heists (New Mode)
•	15-minute rounds
•	Multiple capture points
•	Reputation: Mutant Experience Points (MREP) per owned scoring station granted to the person that captures the scoring station
•	Owned scoring stations produce MREP and the owning team at the count of that round is applied to the faction that owns it. 
•	All active badges are rewarded with MREP from the payout based on their player class count. (see player class section for more information)
•	Real-time territory tracking
Secondary Missions (Side Hustles)
•	Randomly generated, story-driven side hustle missions that can be run multiple times after a short cooldown
•	Uses orange box M5Dial widgets ("Burner Phones") — multiple widgets can run missions in parallel

Mission Flow:
1.	Scan your player badge on the widget to identify yourself
2.	Scan a mission card — the device starts a Heist mission and randomly assigns 4 Points of Interest (POI) to visit
3.	At each POI, scan the correct resource tag (WEAPONS, SECURITY, VEHICLE, or MONEY — one per location)
4.	Return to base and scan the Mission Completion card to submit your mission and earn MREP

Difficulty & Rewards:
•	Each location visited increases your difficulty level and MREP reward
•	More locations secured = higher difficulty = better rewards
•	You have 30 minutes to complete a mission
•	If time runs out after visiting at least 1 location, your earned difficulty is locked — scan the Completion card to still collect your reward
•	If time runs out with zero locations visited, the mission is forfeit and must be restarted

Game Terms and Definitions

•	Side Mission: Orange box widgets (Burner Phones) at base. Scan your badge, then a mission card to start a Heist. Visit 4 POIs and scan the correct resource at each location. Return to base and scan the Completion card within 30 minutes to claim your MREP reward.

Frequently Asked Questions
General Gameplay
Q: How do I win the game?
A: The faction with the most Mutant Experience Points (MREP) at the end of the event wins.
Q: What class should I start with? 
A: Everyone starts as a Hustler. 
•	Class Progression: Hustler → Worker → Enforcer → Capo → Shot Caller → Lieutenant → Under Boss → Boss → Kingpin → Legend.
Q: How do I earn Mutant Experience Points (MREP)? 
A: Through territory control, completing missions, turning in props.
Class System
Q: Can I change my class during the game? 
A: Yes, but you must progress through the levels and meet all requirements for the new class.
Q: How many medics can each team have? 
A: Medics are limited to 2 per side
Q: What happens if I lose my class markings? 
A: Report to your base referee immediately for replacement markings.
Missions & Objectives
Q: How often do missions reset? 
A: Secondary missions (side hustles) can be run as often as you like. The cooldown is tied to your player badge and the specific mission card used on that widget — use a different mission card or wait for the cooldown to reset. Territory control (Domination Heists) scores every 15 minutes.
Q: Can I run multiple missions simultaneously? 
A: Yes, but each mission type has its own limitations and cooldowns.
Q: What happens if I don't complete a mission in time? 
A: It depends on your progress:
•	If you visited at least 1 location before time ran out, your earned difficulty is locked on the device. Scan the Mission Completion card at base to still submit and collect your MREP reward.
•	If you visited zero locations, the mission is forfeit. The device will hold on a timeout screen until a referee resets it or you scan a reset card. You may then start a fresh mission.
Technical Systems
Q: What if my badge isn't working? 
A: Visit the tech support station at your base for immediate assistance.
Q: How do I join the Discord server? 
A: Instructions are provided during registration, including unique team-specific invite links.
Q: What happens if my app crashes during a mission? 
A: Report to a referee who can verify your progress and help restore your status.
Equipment & Props

A: For this event YES, you can use your own paint. Excluding Monster Ball or red-fill paints. 
Q: What happens if I find a prop? 
A: Bring it to your base referee for scanning and points allocation.
Q: How do vehicle eliminations work? 
A: Vehicles require 2 scenario slug hits - first to disable, second to destroy.
